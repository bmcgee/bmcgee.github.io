Aspects of motion graphics

  1. Voice over
    1. Voice over, what are we trying to say, what information are we trying to convey to the viewer.
    2. Begins with a script, typically a writer.
    3. Usually the main pillar.
  2. On screen picture – Visual look and movement.
    1. After scripting phase typically storyboarding begins. Basic sketches on what scenes and shots will look like within.
    2. Establishes identity and visual brand of piece.
    3. In motion graphics, the first half of this sometime a specialized skill, an art director or designer.
    4. Animation. To hold the screen long enough and to use the medium to its potential, the motion graphics need to move. Usually done by an animator.
  3. Music, sound.
    1. As important as the other two. Used to add emotion and signal change and set tempo and pacing.
    2. Sound effects help change scenes.
    3. Stings - periods at end of sentences of moments in motion graphics. Sound designer or composer
  4. Getting it done.
    1. Things have to be completed or you have nothing. Things need to be coordinated . A producer.
